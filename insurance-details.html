Life Cover


Life is certainly for living but while we are busy living it, we tend to forget about the unforeseen events that could leave dependants struggling to make ends meet. An untimely death can result in exorbitant uncovered expenses and a dramatic reduction in the family income. You need to be sure that you and your family are adequately covered against such a contingency. After all, in the event of your death, you want to be sure that your loved ones are well provided for financially – that they wont have to sacrifice their standard of living or be left with debts to cover, particularly if you are the main breadwinner and they have always relied on your income.


Think about it for a moment. Imagine if your children had to give up the benefits of a good education because there was not enough funds to cover such costs or what if your family home had to be sold because there was insufficient funds to meet bond obligations? Consider your family’s future living expenses, which include annual recurring expenses, as well as infrequent capital expenditure such as buying a new motor vehicle. This is just some of the reasons why knowing how much risk protection and life assurance you need is such an important factor to consider when planning your finances.


The insurers we are affiliated to are able to provide you with the necessary life cover, however it must be stressed that a summary or detailed financial needs analysis must be undertaken prior to the advocating of the appropriate solutions.

Most Asked Questions

1. What is life insurance?

Life insurance is a contract between an insured person and an insurance company, where the insurer pays a designated beneficiary a sum of money upon the death of the insured person.

2. What are the types of life insurance?

Common types include term life insurance, whole life insurance, and universal life insurance.

3. What is term life insurance?

Term life insurance provides coverage for a specified period (e.g., 10, 20, 30 years) and pays a death benefit if the insured dies within that term.

4. What is whole life insurance?

Whole life insurance provides lifelong coverage and includes an investment component known as the policy's cash value.

5. What is universal life insurance?

Universal life insurance is a type of permanent life insurance with flexible premiums and death benefits and includes a savings element that earns interest.

6. How much life insurance do I need?

The amount of life insurance needed depends on factors such as your financial obligations, income, debts, and the needs of your dependents.
